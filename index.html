The Capital: Antananarivo

Antananarivo , also known by its colonial shorthand form Tana, is the capital and largest city of Madagascar. The larger urban area surrounding the city, known as Antananarivo-Renivohitra , is the capital of Analamanga region. The city sits at 1,280 m (4,199 ft) above sea level in the center of the island, the highest national capital by elevation among the island countries.

01 INTRODUCTION

The Sustainable Business for Africa Platform is a cornerstone of the investment climate component of the External Investment Plan.  It provides EU staff with guidelines and technical assistance to engage in structured dialogue with the local and European private sector in Africa. By doing that, the European Union wants to support the identification of the constraints weighing on the private sector and refraining investment.

02 policy Background

Private sector engagement is a key element of the European Union’s policy for international partnerships and development. In 2014, the European Union published a communication recognizing the private sector as a privileged partner to create jobs, contribute to poverty alleviation, support innovation and technology transfer, and bring financial resources to EU partner countries.
‍
In 2017, the European Union adopted the European Investment Plan, to remove constraints to private investment and support priority reforms in Africa and the EU neighbourhood countries.

04 SOUTH AFRICA

South Africa, officially the Republic of South Africa, is the southernmost country in Africa. With over 58 million people, it is the world's 24th-most populous nation and covers an area of 1,221,037 kilometres. South Africa has three designated capital cities; executive Pretoria, judicial Bloemfontein and legislative Cape Town.

05 UGANDA

Uganda, officially the Republic of Uganda is a landlocked country in East-Central Africa. It is bordered to the east by Kenya, to the north by South Sudan, to the west by the Democratic Republic of the Congo, to the south-west by Rwanda, and to the south by Tanzania. The southern part of the country includes a substantial portion of Lake Victoria, shared with Kenya and Tanzania. Uganda is in the African Great Lakes region.

06 TOGO

Togo is a country in West Africa bordered by Ghana to the west, Benin to the east and Burkina Faso to the north. The country extends south to the Gulf of Guinea, where its capital Lomé is located. Togo covers 57,000 square kilometres, making it one of the smallest countries in Africa, with a population of approximately 7.9 million, as well as one of the narrowest countries in the world with a width of less than 115 km between Ghana and its slightly larger eastern neighbor, Benin.
